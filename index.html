Advice:

Hyperglycaemia in critically unwell adults with COVID-19

Patients admitted to Critical Care with COVID-19 appear to have a higher prevalence of diabetes. Escalation of Critical Care capacity, a need for PPE and large patient numbers may increase the risk of harm from IV insulin.

Higher threshold for starting IV insulin than usual
If blood glucose > 12 mmol/L recheck in 1-2 hours
If blood glucose remains > 12 mmol/L start IV insulin
Target blood glucose range 6-12 mmol/L
Using 50 units Actrapid in 50mls saline (prefilled where available)
Calculate starting rate
Using calculator at saferinsulin.org, app or local version
Record calculator reference code in casenotes unless other clinical priorities
Use paper VRII if not trained
Check blood glucose every 1-2 hours
Whilst IV insulin requires 1 hourly checks, should other clinical
demands take priority this can safely be extended to 2 hourly
Calculate dose adjustment
Using calculator or from paper VRII if not trained
Hypoglycaemia is a clinical emergency
Ensure PRN 50ml of 50% glucose or alternative emergency treatment
is easily accessible at all times where IV insulin is being delivered.
